Body Effect

the voltage at the body terminal (which is usually connected to the source) affects the threshold voltage of the device. This occurs because of the depletion region that forms between the body and channel regions. When a voltage is applied to the body, it changes the width of this depletion region, which alters the effective thickness of the oxide layer between gate and channel.

$$ \begin{aligned} \eta &= \frac{g_m}{g_{mb}} \\ &=\dfrac{\partial V_{TH}}{\partial V_{in}} \\ &= \frac{\partial V_t}{\partial V_{SB}} \end{aligned} $$
